Spring 2026 Data Science Course Project
Medical Data Mining, Descriptive Analytics, and Predictive Analytics

Dear Students,

This semester, all project groups will work on the same medical dataset and develop a complete data science pipeline including data understanding, preprocessing, descriptive analytics, and predictive analytics. Compared to previous years, the project structure is simplified. There will be only two graded milestones:

1. Progress Presentation
2. Final Presentation

Your project grade will be based primarily on your performance in these two presentations, including the technical quality of your work, the clarity of your explanations, the depth of your analysis, the quality of your visualizations, and your ability to answer questions during the presentation.

Common Dataset

All groups must use the following dataset for the project:

Diabetes 130-US Hospitals for Years 1999–2008
UCI Machine Learning Repository

This dataset contains 101,766 hospital encounters, 47 features, mixed categorical and integer attributes, and missing values. It is explicitly suitable for classification and clustering. It also includes early readmission as a natural classification target and variables such as time_in_hospital that may be used for optional regression analysis. ([UCI Makine Öğrenimi Deposu][1])

Project Objective

Your objective is to perform a complete data science study on this medical dataset. The project must include the following components:

1. Data understanding and preprocessing
   You should examine the dataset carefully, identify feature types, analyze missing values, detect data quality problems, justify preprocessing steps, and prepare the final analytical dataset.

2. Descriptive analytics
   You must apply clustering and association rule mining methods to discover meaningful patterns in the data.

Mandatory clustering methods:

* k-means
* hierarchical clustering
* DBSCAN

You are encouraged to apply additional clustering methods if you wish.

Mandatory association rule mining method:

* Apriori

Your goal is to identify interesting and meaningful descriptive patterns that could be useful in a healthcare or hospital-management context.

3. Predictive analytics
   You must formulate and solve at least one predictive problem using the dataset.

Mandatory predictive task:

* classification

Recommended classification target:

* readmitted / early readmission

You must apply feature selection methods and compare multiple predictive models.

You should use at least 3 different classification algorithms and perform 6 to 10 experiments in total by varying:

* algorithm type
* hyperparameters
* feature subsets
* preprocessing choices where appropriate

You are strongly encouraged to try algorithms such as:

* k-NN
* Naive Bayes
* SVM
* Decision Trees
* Random Forest
* Gradient Boosting / XGBoost if available
* ANN / MLP

* formulate and solve a regression task, for example by predicting a numeric variable such as time_in_hospital

Final Presentation
Week 16
Friday, June 5

By the final presentation, your group is expected to complete the full project.

Your final presentation must include:

1. Dataset understanding and preprocessing

* full data cleaning and preprocessing pipeline
* final selected attributes
* explanation of transformations and encoding steps

2. Descriptive analytics

* clustering with k-means, hierarchical clustering, and DBSCAN
* comparison and interpretation of clustering results
* Apriori association rule mining results
* discussion of the most interesting and meaningful patterns

3. Predictive analytics

* feature selection using several methods
* ranked feature tables
* at least 6 to 10 predictive experiments
* at least 3 different classification algorithms
* comparison of evaluation metrics
* confusion matrix of the best model
* ROC curve for different models if applicable
* statistical significance analysis between the best model and the closest competitor
* interpretation of results

4. Conclusions

* what you learned from the descriptive analysis
* what you learned from the predictive analysis
* practical insights you obtained from the medical data
* limitations of your work
* possible future improvements

For the final presentation, prepare a presentation in PDF format named:

CSE4062S26_GroupX_FinalPresentation.pdf

The final presentation should be more complete and detailed than the progress presentation. A maximum of 20 slides is recommended.
